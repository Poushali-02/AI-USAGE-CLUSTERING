Abstract

This study presents a comprehensive analysis of AI assistant usage patterns among students using unsupervised machine learning techniques. Through K-Means clustering and Principal Component Analysis (PCA), we identified distinct behavioral patterns in how students interact with AI tools across different academic disciplines and task types. Our findings reveal significant correlations between satisfaction ratings, assistance levels, and final outcomes, providing insights for educational technology development.
